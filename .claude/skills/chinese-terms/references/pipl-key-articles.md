## Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) - Key Articles

**Adopted:** August 20, 2021
**Effective:** November 1, 2021
**Presidential Order:** No. 91

---

## Chapter 1: General Provisions

### Article 5 - Principles of Personal Information Processing
Personal information processing shall follow the principles of legality, legitimacy, necessity, and good faith.

### Article 6 - Consent Requirement
Personal information shall be processed with the **informed consent** of the individual, except where laws provide otherwise.

### Article 7 - Public Interest Exception
Processing without consent is permitted for:
- Contract performance
- Public health emergencies
- News reporting
- Public interest purposes

---

## Chapter 2: Processing Rules

### Article 13 - Notice Requirements
Personal information processors must **clearly inform** individuals of:
- Name/contact of processor
- Purpose of processing
- Type of information
- Method of processing
- Manner and means of exercising rights

### Article 14 - Consent Standards
Consent must be:
- **Informed** (clear, understandable)
- **Voluntary** (no coercion)
- **Specific** (purpose-limited)
- **Explicit** (affirmative action)

### Article 17 - Sensitive Personal Information
Processing sensitive personal information (biometric, religious, health, financial) requires:
- **Specific purpose**
- **Sufficient necessity**
- **Strict protection measures**
- **Separate informed consent**

### Article 24 - Cross-Border Transfers
Cross-border transfers require:
- Security assessment by CAC (for large processors)
- Certification by qualified institutions
- Standard contracts prescribed by CAC
- Other conditions specified by CAC

## Chapter 4: Data Subject Rights

### Article 44 - Right to Access and Copy
Individuals have the right to access and copy their personal information.

### Article 45 - Right to Correction
Individuals may request correction of inaccurate personal information.

### Article 46 - Right to Deletion
Personal information must be deleted when:
- Processing purpose is achieved
- Consent is withdrawn
- Processing is unlawful
- Individual withdraws consent

### Article 47 - Right to Withdraw Consent
Individuals may withdraw consent at any time.

### Article 48 - Right to Explanation
Individuals may request explanation of processing rules.

### Article 49 - Automated Decision Rights
Individuals have rights regarding automated decision-making, including the right to refuse.

### Article 50 - Right to Complain
Individuals may complain to personal information processors and regulatory authorities.

---

## Chapter 5: Obligations of Processors

### Article 51 - Compliance Measures
Processors must establish:
- Internal rules and procedures
- Personnel management
- Technical security measures

### Article 52 - Personnel Designation
Processors handling large quantities must designate a **Personal Information Protection Officer**.

### Article 53 - Risk Assessment
Prior to the following activities, processors must conduct **personal information protection impact assessment**:
- Processing sensitive personal information
- Using automated decision-making
- Cross-border transfers
- Other high-risk processing

### Article 57 - Data Breach Notification
In case of data breach, processors must:
- Take remedial measures immediately
- Notify individuals and authorities
- Provide risk assessment and remediation
