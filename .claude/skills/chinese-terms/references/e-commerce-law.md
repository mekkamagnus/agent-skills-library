# E-Commerce Law Key Provisions

**Source:** E-Commerce Law of the PRC (2019)
**Last Accessed:** 2025-02-28
**Language:** English

## E-Commerce Law of the PRC

**Adopted:** August 31, 2018
**Effective:** January 1, 2019

---

## Key Articles for Digital Platforms

### Article 10: Market Entity Registration
E-commerce operators must register as market entities (办理市场主体登记).
**Exception:** Small sellers with only occasional transactions and low annual income.

### Article 17: Operator Obligations
E-commerce operators must:
- Protect personal information
- Verify supplier qualifications
- Ensure commodity quality
- Protect consumer rights
- Maintain fair competition

### Article 17: Data and User Protection
Platforms must:
- Protect user information
- Not distort/interfere with user data
- Provide data export capability upon user request
- Delete user data upon account closure

### Article 18: Continuing Obligations
Platforms must:
- Establish rules for transaction security
- Protect consumer rights
- Mediate disputes
- Manage intellectual property complaints
- Protect personal information

### Article 21: Prohibited Practices
Platforms and merchants **shall not:**
- Fabricate user reviews
- Mislead with fake transactions
- Engage in false promotions
- Harm competitors through improper means

### Article 32: IP Protection Mechanism
Platforms must establish **intellectual property protection rules** including:
- Complaint submission process
- Notice-and-takedown mechanism
- Response to right holder complaints
- Liability rules for infringement

### Article 38: Platform Liability for Intellectual Property
If platform knowingly or should know about IP infringement and fails to take action:
- **Joint and several liability** with merchant
- Must take necessary measures upon notice

### Article 49: Right to Cancel Orders
Consumers may cancel orders for:
- Goods purchased online (7-day right)
- Any reason or no reason
**Exceptions:** Customized products, perishables, digital downloads

## Platform-Specific Requirements

### For E-commerce Platforms (like WeChat Mini Programs)

|-------------|-------------|

## Consumer Protection Provisions

### 7-Day Right of Return (Article 49)
- Applies to online purchases
- Consumer may return without reason
- Merchant must honor within 7 days
- Exceptions apply (customized, perishable, digital)

### Disclosure Requirements (Article 17-18)
Platforms must disclose:
- Fee structure
- Refund policy
- Delivery terms
- After-sales service

### Dispute Resolution (Article 58-63)
- Platform must provide dispute mediation
- Consumer rights protection mechanisms
- Refund processing requirements

---

## Penalty Framework

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| No market registration | Order to register + fine |
| IP infringement (knowing) | Joint liability with merchant |
| Fake reviews | Order to correct + fine up to ¥500,000 |
| Failure to protect data | Administrative fines |
| Blocking data export | Administrative fines |

## Key Compliance Requirements for Digital Platforms

### 1. User Agreement & Privacy Policy
- Clear terms of service
- Privacy policy accessible
- Data handling explained
- User rights disclosed

### 2. Merchant Verification
- Verify business license for merchants
- Track merchant compliance
- Remove non-compliant merchants

### 3. Transaction Records
- Maintain for 3+ years
- Available to authorities
- Include transaction details

### 4. IP Complaint System
- Clear complaint process
- Response to notices
- Takedown mechanism
- Merchant reinstatement process

### 5. Consumer Protection
- 7-day return policy honored
- Refund processing
- Dispute mediation
- After-sales service

## Relationship with Other Laws

The E-Commerce Law works with:
- **Consumer Rights Protection Law** - 7-day returns, penalties
- **PIPL** - Data protection requirements
- **Cybersecurity Law** - Security requirements
- **Advertising Law** - Marketing restrictions

**Hierarchy:** Special law (E-Commerce) > General law (Civil Code)
