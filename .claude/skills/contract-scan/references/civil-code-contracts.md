# Civil Code Contract Law (Articles 471-584)

**Source:** https://flk.npc.gov.cn/
**Last Accessed:** 2025-02-28
**Language:** English

## Civil Code of the PRC - Contract Provisions (Articles 471-584)

**Effective:** January 1, 2021

---

## Contract Formation (Articles 471-500)

### Article 471: Methods of Contract Conclusion
A contract may be concluded by **making an offer and acceptance** or by other means.

### Article 472: Definition of Offer
An offer is a manifestation of intention to enter into a contract with another person and must meet the following conditions:
1. The content is specific and definite
2. It indicates that the offeror will be bound upon acceptance

### Article 473: Invitation to Offer
An invitation to offer includes auction announcements, tender announcements, commercial advertisements, and public price announcements.
Commercial advertisements are treated as offers if their content meets the requirements of an offer.

### Article 479: Definition of Acceptance
Acceptance is a manifestation of intention by the offeree to agree to the offer.

### Article 480: Method of Acceptance
Acceptance must be made by **notice** to the offeror, except as otherwise provided by law or agreed by the parties.

### Article 483: Time of Contract Formation
A contract is formed at the time when the **acceptance becomes effective**.

### Article 490: Contract by Performance
Where parties perform obligations before a contract is formed in written form, the contract is formed when one party has **completed performance of its main obligation** and the other has accepted it.

### Article 496: Standard Terms
Where standard terms are used, the party providing them shall:
1. Follow the principle of fairness
2. Reasonably determine the rights and obligations
3. **Promptly call attention** to exclusion or limitation clauses
4. Explain such clauses upon the other party's request

**Invalidity:** Standard terms that exclude or limit liability for personal injury or intentional misconduct are **void**.

### Article 497: Unfair Standard Terms
Standard terms that:
1. Unreasonably exempt the providing party from liability
2. Increase the other party's obligations
3. Exclude the other party's main rights

Are **void and unenforceable**.

## Contract Performance (Articles 509-534)

### Article 509: Performance Principle
 Parties shall fully perform their obligations according to the agreement.
 shall follow the principles of **good faith** and **fair dealing**.

### Article 527: Exercise of Defense Rights
Where a party discovers evidence that the other party cannot perform obligations, it may **suspend performance**, but shall promptly notify the other party.

## Breach of Contract & Liability (Articles 577-584)

### Article 577: General Liability for Breach
Where a party **fails to perform** its obligations or performs them in a manner not in conformity with the agreement, it shall bear liability for breach by:
1. Continuing to perform
2. Taking remedial measures
3. Compensating for losses

### Article 578: Anticipatory Breach
Where a party clearly indicates or demonstrates by its conduct that it will not perform, the other party may claim liability before performance is due.

### Article 579: Monetary Debt Performance
Where a party fails to pay price, remuneration, rent, or interest, the other party may request payment.

### Article 580: Non-Monetary Debt Performance
Where performance is not possible:
1. **Legally impossible**
2. **Factually impossible** (unsuitable for forced performance)
3. **Excessively costly**

Performance is not required, and damages may be awarded instead.

### Article 581: Substitute Performance
Where a third party performs the obligation due to breach, the breaching party shall pay the costs.

### Article 582: Defective Performance Remedies
For defective performance, remedies include:
- Repair (修理)
- Rework (重作)
- Replacement (更换)
- Return (退货)
- Price reduction (减少价款)

### Article 583: Additional Damages
After performance or remedial measures, if the other party still suffers losses, the breaching party shall compensate.

### Article 584: Damages Measurement ⭐ KEY
> "Where a party fails to perform its obligations... causing losses to the other party, the amount of damages shall be **equivalent to the losses caused by the breach**, including the **benefits obtainable after performance**; however, damages shall **not exceed the losses that the breaching party foresaw or should have foreseen** at the time of conclusion of the contract."

**Key Principles:**
1. **Full Compensation:** Actual losses + expected benefits
2. **Foreseeability Limit:** Only losses foreseeable at contracting
3. **No Punitive Damages:** Compensatory only (except consumer fraud)

## Statute of Limitations

### Article 188: General Limitation
The limitation period for judicial protection of civil rights is **3 years** from when the person knows or should know of the rights infringement.

### Article 592: Comparative Fault
Where damages result from both parties' breach:
- Each party bears corresponding liability
- May reduce damages against negligent plaintiff

## Key Takeaways for Contract Review

| Issue | Civil Code Position |
|-------|-------------------|
| Written contract required? | No, oral contracts valid (but hard to prove) |
| Standard terms | Must be called to attention; unfair terms void |
| Breach liability | Strict liability (no fault required) |
| Damages | Actual losses + expected benefits (foreseeable) |
| Specific performance | Available unless impossible/excessively costly |
| Force majeure | Exempts liability if performance impossible |
