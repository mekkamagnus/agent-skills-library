# Commercial Franchise Regulations

**Source:** Measures for the Administration of Commercial Franchise (SAMR)
**Last Accessed:** 2025-02-28
**Language:** English

## Commercial Franchise Administration Measures

**Issued:** February 6, 2007
**Effective:** May 1, 2007
**Authority:** State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR)
**Original:** 商业特许经营管理条例

---

## Key Requirements

### 1. Franchise Qualifications (Article 7)

A franchisor ("特许人") must meet the following conditions:
- **Registered enterprise** (not individual)
- **Owns resources** that can be franchised:
  - Trademark, patent, or other intellectual property
  - Mature business model
  - Ability to provide operational guidance
- **2+ directly operated stores** with **1+ year** of profitable operation

### 2. Franchise Filing (Recordal) - Article 8

Franchisors must file with SAMR (or local commerce bureau) **within 15 days** of signing the **first** franchise.

**Required for filing:**
- Business license
- Franchise operation manual
- Market plan
- Proof of 2+ directly operated stores
- Information about franchise system

### 3. Information Disclosure Requirements - Article 21

Franchisor must provide written disclosure **at least 30 days before** signing agreement or receiving payment.

**Must disclose:**

#### Basic Information
- Name, address, legal representative
- Registration date, registration number
- Trademark, patent registration
- Franchise fees and charges

#### Financial Information
- Financial statements (past 2 years) audited
- Tax status
- Litigation/arbitration history
- Administrative penalties

#### Operational Information
- Number of franchised outlets
- Details of directly operated stores
- Training programs
- Supply chain information
- Franchisee performance

#### Contract Terms
- Summary of franchise agreement terms
- Rights and obligations
- Fees and payment terms
- Territorial exclusivity
- Renewal and termination conditions

### 4. Cooling-Off Period - Article 12

Franchisees have the right to **terminate the franchise agreement within a reasonable time** after signing.

**Standard practice:** 7-day cooling-off period is industry norm.

### 5. Franchise Agreement Requirements - Article 11

A franchise agreement **must be in writing** and include:

1. **Basic Information**
   - Parties, addresses, legal representatives

2. **Franchise Rights**
   - Authorization scope
   - Trademark/patent usage
   - Territorial exclusivity (if any)

3. **Fees and Payments**
   - Initial franchise fee
   - Royalties/continuing fees
   - Marketing fees
   - Payment schedule

4. **Obligations**
   - Franchisor obligations:
     - Training
     - Operational guidance
     - Quality control
     - Supply of materials
     - Brand protection
   - Franchisee obligations:
     - Follow operational standards
     - Maintain quality
     - Protect trade secrets
     - Not compete (if agreed)

5. **Contract Term and Renewal**
   - Duration (typically 3-5 years)
   - Renewal conditions
   - Notice requirements

6. **Termination**
   - Grounds for termination
   - Notice periods
   - Post-termination obligations

## Franchisee Red Flags

| Issue | Risk Level |
|-------|------------|
| Franchisor not registered enterprise | **HIGH** - Cannot legally franchise |
| <2 directly operated stores | **HIGH** - Violates Article 7 |
| <1 year of operation | **HIGH** - Violates Article 7 |
| No SAMR filing | **MEDIUM** - Administrative violation |
| No 30-day disclosure | **HIGH** - Violates Article 21 |
| Unreasonable exclusivity | **MEDIUM** - May be unenforceable |
| Hidden fees/charges | **HIGH** - Disclosure violation |
| No cooling-off period | **MEDIUM** - May be required locally |

## Common Violations & Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Franchising without qualifications | Fine ¥10,000-500,000 |
| Failure to file | Fine ¥10,000-100,000 |
| Failure to disclose | Fine ¥10,000-100,000 |
| Misleading disclosure | Fine ¥50,000-300,000 |
| Unfair contract terms | Order to correct + fine |
