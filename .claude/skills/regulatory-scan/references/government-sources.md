# Key Government Sources for Regulatory Monitoring

**Source:** Compiled from official Chinese government websites
**Last Accessed:** 2025-02-28
**Language:** English

## Primary Government Agencies to Monitor

---

## 1. NPC (全国人大) - National People's Congress

**Focus:** New laws, Civil Code amendments
**Website:** https://flk.npc.gov.cn/
**Language:** Chinese (some English translations)

**What to Monitor:**
- New legislation
- Civil Code amendments
- Major legal reforms
- Supreme Court interpretations

**Key Laws for Digital Platforms:**
- Civil Code (民法典) - Contracts, Tort, Property
- Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL, 个人信息保护法)
- Data Security Law (数据安全法)
- Cybersecurity Law (网络安全法)
- E-commerce Law (电子商务法)
- Consumer Rights Protection Law (消费者权益保护法)

---

## 2. CAC (网信办) - Cyberspace Administration of China

**Focus:** Data/cyber regulations, PIPL implementation
**Website:** https://www.cac.gov.cn/
**Language:** Chinese

**What to Monitor:**
- PIPL implementation guidance
- Data export rules
- Cross-border transfer requirements
- Algorithm regulations
- Content moderation requirements
- Data security assessments

**Recent Focus Areas:**
- Personal information protection impact assessments
- Cross-border data transfer standards
- Generative AI regulations
- Platform responsibility requirements

---

## 3. MIIT (工信部) - Ministry of Industry and Information Technology

**Focus:** ICP filing, internet regulations, telecommunications
**Website:** https://www.miit.gov.cn/
**Language:** Chinese

**What to Monitor:**
- ICP beiang requirements
- 5G/telecommunications regulations
- Internet service provider rules
- Data center requirements
- APP privacy requirements

**Sub-sites:**
- ICP Filing Portal: https://beian.miit.gov.cn/
- ICP Application: https://icp.beian.miit.gov.cn/

---

## 4. MOE (教育部) - Ministry of Education

**Focus:** Education policy, Double Reduction implementation
**Website:** https://www.moe.gov.cn/
**Language:** Chinese (some English)

**What to Monitor:**
- Double Reduction Policy updates
- Off-campus training regulations
- Online education restrictions
- Student protection rules
- Weekend/holiday training prohibitions

**Key Policy:**
- Opinions on Further Reducing Homework and Off-Campus Training Burdens (2021)
- "5+2" after-school service model
- Non-profit conversion requirements

---

## 5. SAMR (市场监管总局) - State Administration for Market Regulation

**Focus:** Consumer protection, e-commerce, business registration
**Website:** https://www.samr.gov.cn/
**Language:** Chinese

**What to Monitor:**
- E-commerce compliance
- Consumer protection enforcement
- Franchise regulations
- Advertising standards
- Platform responsibility

**Key Areas:**
- E-commerce Law enforcement
- Consumer rights protection
- Anti-monopoly guidelines for platforms
- Franchise disclosure requirements

---

## 6. WeChat Developers

**Focus:** Mini Program policy changes
**Website:** https://developers.weixin.qq.com/
**Language:** Chinese (some English)

**What to Monitor:**
- Mini Program review policies
- Category classification updates
- Payment requirements
- API changes
- Annual certification requirements

**Key Pages:**
- Review Policies: https://developers.weixin.qq.com/miniprogram/product/product_review_policies.html
- Privacy Requirements: https://developers.weixin.qq.com/miniprogram/dev/framework/open-ability/privacy.html
- Open Platform: https://open.weixin.qq.com/

## Alert Triggers — IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

1. **New laws effective** within 30 days
2. **Platform policy changes** affecting submission/approval
3. **Data breach rules** with new requirements
4. **Enforcement actions** announced in your industry
5. **License requirements** introduced for your business type
